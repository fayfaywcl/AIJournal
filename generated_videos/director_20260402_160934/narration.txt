I started in a room that felt like it had already been left behind. One screen was still awake, blue against all the dark glass, while I sat there pretending I had nowhere else to be. Outside, the city looked cold and sealed off, all concrete, wet ground, and windows holding their own light. Then the day opened a little on the water, with distance between me and the shore, and for a moment everything slowed down enough to breathe. By the time the freeway pulled me toward downtown, the city was loud again, moving whether I was ready or not.
